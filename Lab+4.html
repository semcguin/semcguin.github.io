plt.show()

The first graph shows a loosely positive association between household income and student debt. The variance at all incomes is quite high however. This could be attributed to the many types of colleges present in this subset of data. There are small privates and large public schools, meaning a large variation in tuition and the amount and types of financial aid students receive. In addition, different families deal with college payments in different ways. High income families may pay for school outright, in which case students may have lower debt.

However, wealtier families may also send their children to more expensive private schools, and take out loans to do so. This is why we see some heteroskedasticity: as income increases, variation appears to increase. Low income families may receieve so much financial aid their students have low debt, or they may not be so lucky and end up with a large debt burden. Middle income families may not qualify for financial aid, and have to pay more in loans.
